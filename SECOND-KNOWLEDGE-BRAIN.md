## 1. Core Concepts & Frameworks

### 1.1 Technical Diving Decompression Physiology & Safety — Foundational Methods

### 1.1 Decompression physiology
Inert gas (N2/He) uptake/release across tissue compartments; supersaturation → bubble formation → DCS (joint pain, neuro, pulmonary). VGE grading; PFO risk.
### 1.2 Bühlmann ZHL-16C
16 tissue compartments (4-635 min halftimes); M-values (a, b coefficients); gradient factors (GF low/high) for conservatism (e.g. 30/85).
### 1.3 Gases
Nitrox (EANx): MOD by O2 (PO2 <1.4 work/<1.6 deco), CNS% clock; trimix/heliox reduce narcosis (END<30-40 m) & gas density (WOB); hypoxia at deep switch.
### 1.4 Planning & risk
Rule of thirds (1/3 out, 1/3 back, 1/3 reserve); rock-bottom gas; staged decompression; oxygen toxicity; DCS recognition & O2 first aid; emergency gas; fitness-to-dive.
